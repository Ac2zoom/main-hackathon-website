What is HackingEDU?

HackingEDU is the World's Largest Education Hackathon conceived and executed by students, for students, with the intent of disrupting the classroom and really pushing the envelope on the contemporary education system. We are student organizers from over thirteen different universities, including Stanford, UC Berkeley, UCLA, USC, UC Davis, and many more!

Our Mission

Our biggest goal is to inspire the next generation of students to solve the problems that plague our education system. This is an opportunity for us as college students to raise our voice. It’s our time to redefine education. HackingEDU is excited to provide a launchpad for fellow student hackers to invent the future.

"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world."

Learn

Learning is the first step to understanding but we don’t want students to stop there. Our goal is to inspire a new kind of education system that works more effectively than our current one. Gain the skills you’ll need for our hackathon!

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is a 24-36 hour long programming event. Hundreds of passionate students come together, form teams, share ideas, and help each other build innovative applications. It's a fantastic opportunity to meet new people, learn from mentors, and make cool stuff. They're very beginner friendly. Everybody is there to help each other and have fun.

I can't write code but still want to attend. Help?

The hackathon community welcomes you with open arms - plenty of mentors want to help you make your first hack a reality. We'll have mentors on site to teach you lots of new skills and guide you towards creating your first hack. All the students are very friendly and willing to help each other out.

How big are teams?

To be eligible for prizes, teams need to be 1-4 people. If you're okay not being eligible for prizes, you can totally have as many people on your team as you want. Your team members can be from other universities/schools. One of the best things about hackathons is meeting hackers from all over the country. We'll have a time designated for building teams, so don't worry if you come alone!

Who can attend?

Anyone. If you are under 18, we’ll need a parental consent form.

What should I bring?

Your valid student ID, laptop, phone, chargers, sleeping equipment, and lots of energy (You'll need it, trust me). If you’re a minor, a parental consent form too that we’ll email to you.

How do I get there?

More precise information coming soon. We will have buses and shuttles from most major university in California. Don't worry we got you covered.

Workshops

Training Day was a pre-hackathon event full of workshops and hands-on-training at the Paypal Headquarters in San Jose, CA. Speakers Guillermo Rauch and Jacob Thornton, the founders of Socket.io and Twitter Bootstrap respectively, delivered an insightful and inspiring keynote on technology entrepreneurship. Moreover, mentors and tech guides gave talks on various technologies( some being Node.JS, Firebase, Twilio APIs, Pebble, iOS ) and worked with students to help them master the tools they will need to develop their skills in preparation for the main hackathon.
